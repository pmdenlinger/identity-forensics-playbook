# What is Identity Forensics? (for Counsel)

**Identity Forensics (IF)** is a repeatable method for connecting a *person* to their *many digital identities* (work, historic aliases, guest accounts, and personal logins) and tracing how those identities interacted with Microsoft 365 (and adjacent systems). The goal: make sure litigation collections and reviews reflect **everything the custodian actually touched**, not just what their current work mailbox happens to show.

Identity Forensics complements (it doesn’t replace) your normal eDiscovery playbook. It reduces re‑collections, timeline risk, and sanctions exposure by getting **scope right the first time**.

---

## Why counsel should care (in one minute)

- **Cloud work ≠ one mailbox.** In Microsoft 365, people routinely act under *multiple identities*: current UPN, historic UPNs (name/domain changes), **guest** accounts in partner tenants, and sometimes a **personal Microsoft Account (MSA)** on mobile. A mailbox‑only collection can miss Teams posts, files, and links created under these other identities.  
- **Identity is the real perimeter.** Access, audit, retention, and legal hold all apply **through identity** first. If you don’t map identities, you don’t have the full evidence picture.  
- **Defensibility improves.** Showing that your scope accounted for work + guest + historic + personal contexts is strong evidence of a *reasonable and diligent* search if challenged.

---

## Plain‑English definition

> **Identity Forensics** maps a custodian’s relevant accounts (work, historic, guest, and personal) and correlates sign‑ins, memberships, share links, mail rules, and OAuth app grants to determine where potentially responsive evidence exists. The output is a *clear, counsel‑readable* scope and a *defensible evidence bundle*.

---

## Typical problems Identity Forensics solves

- **“Missing Teams messages.”** Posts made while the custodian was a **guest** in a partner team won’t appear if you only collect their home tenant mailbox/Teams.
